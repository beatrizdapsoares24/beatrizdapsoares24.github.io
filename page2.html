Digital Elevation Model (DEM) Map

The following page contains the workings and results of the third lab exploration on Digital Elevation Models. Through this lab, we were required to either construct a 3D Flood model of an urban area of our choice or compare an old map to modern data in a river natural environment. I personally chose to do the 3D urban model since I felt it would be more interesting.

My region of interest (ROI) for this exploration was Amsterdam, the Netherlands. I went with Amsterdam as my urban city of choice due to its relative proximity to the coast, large population and general affluence in the Netherlands as the capital city and a tourism hotspot. The Netherlands is famously known for having reclaimed a lot of the land on which it is built, especially in the coastal regions of the country. As a result, “around 59% of the Dutch land surface is prone to flooding, including 26% that lies below sea level…floods are among the worst disasters that can hit the Netherlands" (PBL Netherlands Environmental Assessment Agency, 2025). The results that came back from my 3D model of Amsterdam, showcasing how much of the city would be flooded, provided sea level rose by 1 meter, were unsurprising since Amsterdam is located 2 meters below sea level.
